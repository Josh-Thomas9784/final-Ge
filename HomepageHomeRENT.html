Home-renter Plan Outline

1.Assess Energy Consumption:


Cost: Free
Benefit: Understanding current energy usage patterns helps identify areas for improvement and sets a baseline for measuring future energy savings.

2.Energy Audit:


Cost: $200 - $500 (professional audit), free (DIY audit)
Benefit: Identifies specific areas of energy inefficiency in the home, allowing for targeted improvements to maximize energy savings.

3.Request Energy-Efficient Appliances and Lighting:


Cost: Potentially no additional cost, or small rental increase
Benefit: Reduced energy bills and increased comfort with energy-efficient appliances provided by the landlord.

4.Install Energy-Efficient Lighting:
Cost: $20 - $100+
Benefit:Lower electricity bills and longer-lasting bulbs, with potential savings over the rental period.

5.Use Smart Plugs and Power Strips:
Cost: $20 - $100+
Benefit: Easily control energy usage of electronics and appliances, reducing standby power consumption and saving on electricity bills.

6.Seal Drafts and Use Draft Stoppers:
Cost: $20 - $50+
Benefit: Benefit: Improved comfort and potential savings on heating and cooling costs by preventing air leaks.

7.Discuss Renewable Energy Options with Landlord:
Cost: Varies (potential increase in rent or negotiation for renewable energy sources)
Benefit: Benefit: Access to renewable energy sources such as solar or wind power, potentially reducing environmental impact and energy costs.

8.Conserve Water:
Cost: Free to minimal cost (behavioral changes)
Benefit: Reduced water bills, conservation of fresh water resources, and potential savings of hundreds to thousands of dollars annually.

Total Cost Range: $60 - $350+ (excluding potential rent increases & incentives and rebates)

Overall Benefits: Lower utility bills and potential savings over the rental period. - Increased comfort and control over energy usage. - Reduced environmental impact through energy conservation and potential use of renewable energy sources. - Potential for a more sustainable living environment with minimal upfront investment. By following this plan, renters can implement green energy practices within their rented homes, contributing to energy efficiency and environmental sustainability while enjoying potential cost savings. It's important for renters to communicate with landlords about proposed changes and to seek permission before making alterations to the property.
